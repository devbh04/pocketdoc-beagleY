# Emergency Guidance and Red Flags

This document highlights critical clinical symptoms ("red flags") that represent immediate medical emergencies. In these situations, do not wait for home remedies or offline assistants -- seek emergency medical services (EMS) immediately.

## Cardiovascular System
### Chest Pain or Pressure
- **Symptoms:** Sudden chest pain, pressure, squeezing, or fullness in the center of the chest. The pain may radiate to the jaw, neck, back, or one or both arms.
- **Associated signs:** Shortness of breath, cold sweats, lightheadedness, or nausea.
- **Action:** Call emergency services (911 or local equivalent) immediately. Do NOT drive yourself to the hospital.

### Signs of Stroke (F.A.S.T.)
- **Face Drooping:** Does one side of the face droop or is it numb? Ask the person to smile. Is the smile uneven?
- **Arm Weakness:** Is one arm weak or numb? Ask the person to raise both arms. Does one arm drift downward?
- **Speech Difficulty:** Is speech slurred? Is the person unable to speak or hard to understand? Ask the person to repeat a simple sentence.
- **Time to call:** If the person shows any of these symptoms, even if the symptoms go away, call emergency services immediately.

---

## Respiratory System
### Severe Breathing Difficulties
- **Symptoms:** Inability to speak in full sentences without gasping for breath, breathing that is unusually rapid or shallow, lips or fingernails turning blue/gray (cyanosis), or chest drawing in deeply during breathing (retractions).
- **Action:** Call emergency services immediately. Sit the person upright.

---

## Nervous System
### Loss of Consciousness or Altered Mental State
- **Symptoms:** Fainting and not waking up within 1-2 minutes, sudden severe confusion, slurred speech, sudden inability to move a limb, or active seizures.
- **Action:** Call emergency services. Place the person in the recovery position (on their side) if they are breathing, clear their airway, and monitor them.

---

## Anaphylaxis (Severe Allergic Reaction)
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty breathing, swelling of the tongue or throat, hives spreading rapidly, dizziness, drop in blood pressure, or vomiting after exposure to a known allergen (e.g., peanuts, bee stings, medications).
- **Action:** Administer an Epinephrine Auto-Injector (EpiPen) into the outer thigh immediately if available. Call emergency services immediately.
