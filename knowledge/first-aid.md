# First Aid Protocols

This document outlines basic first aid protocols for burns, cuts, choking, CPR, and sprains.

## Burns
### Minor Burns (1st and 2nd Degree, under 3 inches)
- **Cooling:** Immediately run cool (not cold) tap water over the burn for 10-15 minutes or apply a clean, cool, damp compress. Do NOT use ice, butter, or ointments.
- **Protection:** Cover the burn loosely with a sterile, non-stick gauze bandage. Avoid breaking any blisters.
- **Pain Relief:** Over-the-counter pain relievers like acetaminophen or ibuprofen can help reduce swelling and discomfort.

### Major Burns (3rd Degree or large burns)
- **Action:** Call emergency services immediately.
- **Treatment:** Do NOT remove burned clothing stuck to skin. Do NOT immerse large severe burns in cold water. Elevate the burned area above heart level if possible and cover loosely with a clean, dry, sterile cloth.

## Cuts and Wounds
### Minor Cuts, Scrapes, and Scratches
- **Cleanliness:** Wash your hands with soap and water first.
- **Bleeding Control:** Apply gentle pressure with a clean cloth or sterile bandage until bleeding stops.
- **Cleaning:** Rinse the wound with clear, cool water. Wash around the wound with soap, but avoid getting soap directly in the wound to prevent irritation.
- **Protection:** Apply a thin layer of petroleum jelly or antibiotic ointment (e.g., Neosporin) and cover with an adhesive bandage or sterile gauze.

### Severe Bleeding
- **Action:** Call emergency services.
- **Pressure:** Apply direct pressure to the wound with a clean cloth or bandage. Elevate the wound above the heart if possible. Keep pressure applied continuously until medical help arrives.

## Choking
### Adult or Child (Conscious)
- **Identify:** Look for signs of choking (inability to speak, clutching throat, weak coughing).
- **Abdominal Thrusts (Heimlich Maneuver):**
  1. Stand behind the person and wrap your arms around their waist.
  2. Make a fist with one hand and place the thumb side slightly above the navel.
  3. Grasp your fist with your other hand and press into the abdomen with quick, upward thrusts.
  4. Repeat until the object is expelled or the person becomes unconscious.

## Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR)
### Adults (Hands-Only CPR for untrained lay responders)
- **Safety:** Verify the scene is safe, tap the shoulder, and ask loudly: "Are you okay?"
- **Action:** If unresponsive and not breathing (or only gasping), call emergency services and get an AED if available.
- **Compression:**
  - Place the heel of one hand in the center of the chest (lower half of the sternum).
  - Place your other hand on top and interlace your fingers.
  - Push hard and fast in the center of the chest: at least 2 inches deep, at a rate of 100 to 120 compressions per minute (to the beat of the song "Stayin' Alive").
  - Allow the chest to fully recoil between compressions. Continue until professional help arrives or an AED is ready.

## Sprains and Strains
### R.I.C.E. Protocol
- **Rest:** Avoid putting weight on or using the injured joint/limb.
- **Ice:** Apply cold packs or ice wrapped in a thin towel for 15-20 minutes, 4-8 times a day.
- **Compression:** Wrap the injured limb snugly (but not tightly enough to cut off circulation) with an elastic compression bandage.
- **Elevation:** Keep the injured limb elevated above the level of the heart to reduce swelling.
