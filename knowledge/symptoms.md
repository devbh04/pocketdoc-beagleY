# Symptom Guide and Urgency Levels

This document maps common symptoms to potential causes, recommended home actions, and urgency levels.

## Fever
### Overview
A temporary increase in body temperature, often due to an illness or infection.
- **Normal Temperature:** 37°C (98.6°F)
- **Fever Definition:** 38°C (100.4°F) or higher

### Urgency Levels
- **Low (Green):** Fever under 38.9°C (102°F) in adults, responding to fever reducers, with no other severe symptoms.
- **Medium (Yellow):** Fever lasting more than 3 days, or temperature between 38.9°C and 40°C (102°F - 104°F) in adults.
- **High (Orange):** Fever accompanied by stiff neck, confusion, severe headache, breathing difficulties, or persistent vomiting. Seek immediate medical evaluation.
- **Emergency (Red):** Temperature above 40°C (104°F) unresponsive to medication, or any fever in infants under 3 months (above 38°C / 100.4°F). Go to the ER immediately.

### Action Plan
- Stay hydrated (water, electrolytes).
- Rest.
- Take acetaminophen or ibuprofen to reduce fever.

## Headaches
### Tension Headache (Most Common)
- **Sensation:** Dull, aching pain, often described as a tight band around the head.
- **Urgency:** Low.
- **Action:** Rest in a quiet room, apply a cool compress, take mild OTC pain relievers, stay hydrated.

### Migraine Headache
- **Sensation:** Pulsing or throbbing pain, usually on one side of the head, often with nausea or sensitivity to light/sound.
- **Urgency:** Low to Medium.
- **Action:** Rest in a dark, quiet room; take migraine-specific medication or OTC pain relievers.

### Red Flag Headache (Sudden / Severe)
- **Sensation:** "Thunderclap" headache (sudden, severe pain reaching peak within 1 minute), or headache with fever, stiff neck, confusion, double vision, numbness, or difficulty speaking.
- **Urgency:** Emergency (Red). Seek immediate emergency medical care.

## Abdominal Pain
### Mild Cramps or Bloating
- **Sensation:** Generalized, mild pain, gas, or indigestion.
- **Urgency:** Low.
- **Action:** Rest, avoid heavy meals, drink clear fluids. Take antacids or simethicone if appropriate.

### Appendicitis Warning Signs
- **Sensation:** Dull pain starting near the navel and moving to the lower right abdomen, becoming sharp. Often accompanied by loss of appetite, nausea, vomiting, or low fever.
- **Urgency:** Emergency (Red). Do NOT eat or drink, do NOT take laxatives, and go to the nearest ER immediately.

## Rashes and Skin Conditions
### Hives (Urticaria)
- **Sensation:** Red, itchy, raised welts on the skin, often caused by an allergic reaction.
- **Urgency:** Low to Medium.
- **Action:** Take an oral antihistamine (e.g., diphenhydramine or cetirizine), apply cool compresses, avoid scratching.
- **Red Flag:** If hives occur with facial swelling, difficulty breathing, or dizziness, this is anaphylaxis. Urgency is Emergency (Red) - administer Epinephrine (EpiPen) if available and call emergency services immediately.
