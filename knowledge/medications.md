# Over-the-Counter (OTC) Medication Safety Guide

This document provides dosage instructions, safety guidelines, and drug interactions for common over-the-counter medications.

## Acetaminophen (Tylenol)
- **Primary Use:** Pain reliever and fever reducer. Does NOT reduce inflammation.
- **Adult Dosage:**
  - Standard dose: 325 mg to 650 mg every 4 to 6 hours as needed.
  - Maximum daily limit: 3,000 mg to 4,000 mg in 24 hours.
- **Safety Warnings:**
  - **Liver Warning:** Severe liver damage may occur if you take more than the maximum daily limit, take it with other medications containing acetaminophen, or consume 3 or more alcoholic drinks daily while using this product.
  - Check active ingredients on all cold/flu products to avoid accidental double-dosing.

## Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin)
- **Primary Use:** Nonsteroidal Anti-inflammatory Drug (NSAID) used to reduce pain, fever, and inflammation (swelling).
- **Adult Dosage:**
  - Standard dose: 200 mg to 400 mg every 4 to 6 hours as needed.
  - Maximum daily limit: 1,200 mg in 24 hours for self-care (up to 3,200 mg under a doctor's supervision).
- **Safety Warnings:**
  - **Stomach Warning:** NSAIDs can increase the risk of stomach ulcers, bleeding, or irritation. Take with food or milk to reduce stomach upset.
  - **Kidney/Heart Warning:** Long-term use or high doses can affect kidney function and increase risk of high blood pressure or stroke. Consult a doctor if you have kidney disease, heart disease, or are taking blood thinners.

## Antihistamines
### First-Generation (Sedating) - e.g., Diphenhydramine (Benadryl)
- **Primary Use:** Allergic reactions, hives, runny nose, itchy watery eyes, and temporary sleep aid.
- **Adult Dosage:** 25 mg to 50 mg every 4 to 6 hours. Do not exceed 300 mg in 24 hours.
- **Safety Warnings:** Causes marked drowsiness. Do not drive, operate machinery, or consume alcohol while taking it.

### Second-Generation (Non-Drowsy) - e.g., Cetirizine (Zyrtec), Loratadine (Claritin)
- **Primary Use:** Daily relief of seasonal allergy symptoms without causing significant drowsiness.
- **Adult Dosage:**
  - Cetirizine: 10 mg once daily.
  - Loratadine: 10 mg once daily.
- **Safety Warnings:** Less likely to cause drowsiness, but some individuals may still experience mild sedation. Do not exceed recommended doses.
